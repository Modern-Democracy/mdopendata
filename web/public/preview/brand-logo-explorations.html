Logo exploration — compass rose × PEI silhouette

PEI's long east–west crescent is a gift here: it sits naturally across a compass rose's horizontal axis, making the island read as both place and direction. Five directions to consider — same building blocks recombined.

Five directions

1. Island as needle

PEI replaces the east–west arm of the compass rose. A vertical needle and ring complete the rose; a brick dot marks Charlottetown.

Reads as map and instrument simultaneously. The island is the active element.

At very small sizes the island detail blurs into a horizontal stroke.

2. Rose over island

A traditional 8-point compass rose sits where Charlottetown is on the island. PEI is the ground; the rose marks the place.

Clear hierarchy: place + position. Translates to a stamp/seal feel.

Wider aspect ratio — needs a horizontal lockup.

3. Crescent rose

Compass disc as frame, faint 4-point rose behind, PEI in green as foreground. Charlottetown gets a labelled benchmark tick.

Most "instrumental" — feels like a map mark or seal. Strong as a square avatar.

Most detailed of the five; needs minimum 32px before details collapse.

4. Spindle & island

Stripped-back: just the island silhouette and the compass spindle (N–S arrow) running through Charlottetown. No disc, no second arms.

Cleanest. Best app icon. Reads at 16px.

Less obviously a "compass" — leans more navigational than rose.

5. Cartouche

All elements together inside a printed-map cartouche. Rose above, island below, the wordmark engraved as legend. The most "old-survey" of the lot.

Strong as a seal, footer mark, document header, or letterhead.

Too detailed for app icons or favicons — pair with one of the simpler marks above for product chrome.
